We miss you

1. Hellow everyone, during the last maker week Jonas Walravens developer at the Nieuwsblad newsroom and I Robbie Bardijn Javascript dev at team publishing studio focussed ourselves on the We miss you challenge.

2. We want to help our readers in the news apps with creating a read habit by our we miss you mail.

3.Our customer journey hook starts when a reader becomes inactive after 1 week. We'll send him our "We miss you" email as an external trigger accompanied with 2 small features. We will send Unread articles in the mail for that reader and he or she will see how many other readers have read these articles.

The action will be the click in the mail and when that happens we take the user back to the application, behind the screens we'll check if the app is installed.

To increase the tribe feeling as a reward we'll also want to add in a feature in the app that counts all read articles for you as a reader. So you'll actually see how many articles you have read in the app or on the website.

The user will return as investment his Data and Time in the app. Then after he becomes inactive again, the hook can start over.

3. Here you can see a small part of our complete journey. In the first screen on the left you see the we miss you mail coming in in jonas his inbox.

The second screen holds the actual mail with the features I showed you in the customer journey hook, you can see how many times an article was read right next to the glasses icon.

When the user clicks on the article we check if the app is installed and if so we open it. And then we show him the article and increase his read articles by one in the upper right corner.

We also worked on a second e mail we could send out the second week a user is inactive. This one holds features like 5 free articles to give away or to spend on your own.

4. As cherry on the cake we already gathered some data. For the Nieuwsblad brand for example we found around 99.000 non actives users for 1 week. As you can see there really is something to gain here. Credits for this data research go to Ruth Verheyen.
